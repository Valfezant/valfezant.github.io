Llama Fight!

• Game Design
• Programming

Team project
(2 people)

3 weeks
(2025)

Llama Fight! is a 1v1 fighting game where you face your friends in combat as the most ruthless animal on Earth: the llama!

While llamas are widely known as funny and strange creatures, in reality they are also very hardy and violent, with physical conflict being at the base of their social structure. This humorous take on a fighting game centers the entire combat system around the fighting style of llamas, translating the actual nature and behaviours of the animal to game mechanics.

Fights between llamas are centered around throwing the adversary off their balance through kicking and headbutting. They are also known to spit, and the spit content can change depending on their aggression. These formed the basis for the player’s attacks, which consist of headbutts (light attack), kicks (heavy attack), and spit (charged attack).

Team Project

This was a short class assignment, and I was paired up with another designer. I laid out the base concept, then we worked together to expand it.

Unsteady balance

Balance in Llama Fight! serves both as a damage meter and multiplier: a player loses when their balance reaches 0%, but will also be more vulnerable to attacks as it decreases. Unlike health, balance isn’t a hard-set, tangible amount, and so it can be recovered almost instantaneously. After a hit, the player can mash a button or attack their opponent back (revenge) to regain some balance.

Berserk Mode activated!

After 5 successful revenges, the player enters the Berserk mode, inspired by the real "Berserk Llama Syndrome”. During Berserk mode, the player’s damage output will drastically increase, but their balance will steadily drop until it reaches 30%. Immediately afterwards, they will enter a Confusion state, where they will be able to recover balance by mashing a button.

The constant flowing of balance, where each hit is both a significant loss and an opportunity to turn the tides, creates a frenetic and unpredictable rhythm where it feels like each player is always moments away from a catastrophic loss or miraculous triumph. The game encourages quick reaction and impulsive decisions, making for a light-hearted yet engaging experience, turned even more silly and absurd by the heavy reliance on button mashing.
